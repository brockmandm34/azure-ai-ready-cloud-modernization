# CAF Strategy Alignment

## Purpose

The Strategy phase defines why the organization is adopting cloud services and what business outcomes the modernization effort should support.

For Contoso Operations, the cloud strategy is driven by the need to modernize legacy operations, reduce manual work, improve reporting, strengthen security, and prepare for production-ready AI workloads.

## Business Drivers

| Driver | Description |
|---|---|
| Operational efficiency | Reduce manual workflows, duplicate data entry, and email-based approvals |
| AI readiness | Prepare approved data, secure access, and monitoring for AI-enabled workloads |
| Modern reporting | Improve visibility into operations, performance, cost, and business outcomes |
| Security modernization | Improve identity, access, secrets management, and monitoring |
| Resiliency | Reduce dependency on fragile manual processes and unmanaged legacy systems |
| Governance | Establish policies, ownership, and controls before scaling cloud and AI adoption |

## Target Business Outcomes

The modernization strategy should support the following outcomes:

- Faster access to trusted internal information
- Improved workflow consistency and auditability
- Reduced operational delays
- Better visibility into performance and usage
- Stronger security and compliance posture
- A scalable foundation for AI assistants and automation
- A phased migration path that limits business disruption

## Strategic Recommendation

Contoso Operations should pursue a phased Azure modernization strategy focused first on foundational readiness, then priority workload modernization, and finally production AI adoption.

The recommended strategic sequence is:

1. Establish cloud governance and identity foundation.
2. Modernize priority data sources and reporting workflows.
3. Implement monitoring and security posture management.
4. Pilot one high-value AI workload.
5. Scale AI adoption using repeatable patterns and operational controls.
