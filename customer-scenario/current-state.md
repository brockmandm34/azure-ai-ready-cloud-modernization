# Current State

## Organization Profile

Contoso Operations is a fictional regulated, operations-heavy organization that relies on legacy systems, manual workflows, and fragmented reporting to support internal business processes.

The organization wants to adopt Azure AI capabilities but first needs to modernize its infrastructure, data, security, and operational practices.

## Current Technology Environment

The current environment includes:

- Legacy SQL Server databases
- Shared drives used for document storage
- Excel workbooks used for reporting and forecasting
- SharePoint lists used for tracking operational requests
- Manual approval workflows handled through email
- Limited centralized monitoring
- Inconsistent identity and access practices
- Minimal cloud governance structure

## Current Pain Points

### Fragmented Data

Operational data is spread across SQL Server, Excel, SharePoint, and file shares. This makes it difficult to create reliable reporting, automate processes, or prepare data for AI workloads.

### Manual Processes

Many workflows depend on email, spreadsheets, and manual handoffs. This slows down operations and creates inconsistent audit trails.

### Limited Observability

The organization lacks a consistent monitoring and alerting model across applications, data pipelines, and user-facing workflows.

### Security and Governance Gaps

Secrets, access controls, and administrative permissions are not consistently managed. The organization needs stronger identity, access, policy, and monitoring practices before scaling AI workloads.

### AI Readiness Gap

Leadership wants to use AI for internal knowledge search, reporting assistance, and workflow automation. However, the current environment is not ready for production AI because data, identity, monitoring, and governance foundations need improvement.

## Current-State Summary

The organization has business demand for AI but needs a stronger Azure foundation before AI workloads can be deployed securely and reliably at scale.
